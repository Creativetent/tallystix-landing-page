About Us

The idea behind the TallyStix wallet is that information was once inscribed on “Tally Sticks” 30,000 years ago. The fact that tally sticks were still used to record financial transactions in England from the 13th to the 19th century places them among the most resilient storage devices in history. The sizes of the notches represented currency units. The tally stick was a primitive memory aid used to record and document numbers, quantities, and messages (or simply tally). Tallies are now used as currency for a variety of purposes, including messaging, scheduling, and even currency. They have become so common in financial and legal transactions. The decentralized, non-custodial peer-to-peer exchange model used for digital asset leveraging is brought back by the Tallystix wallet. Tokenization, a Web3 platform, and an open source cryptocurrency wallet are all combined in a TallyStix wallet.
